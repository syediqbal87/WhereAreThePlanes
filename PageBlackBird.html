Blackbird Variants

"Blackbirds" is referred to family of airplanes variants, specifically the A-12, SR-71, M-21, and YF-12. The planes are similar but have some key differences.

A-12: Single-seat reconnaissance aircraft developed by the CIA under project OXCART as the U-2 spy plane's successor. It was intended to meet the nation's need for a very fast, very high-flying reconnaissance aircraft that could avoid Soviet air defenses.

SR-71: Dual-seat SR-71 Blackbird was the Air Force's two-seat follow-on version of the OXCART. The SR-71 Blackbird was about six feet longer, weighed an additional 15,000 pounds fully loaded, had a more prominent nose and body chines, had a two-seat cockpit, and carried additional optical and radar imagery systems.

M-21: Mothership for the D-21 reconnaissance drone. It had a pilot and a Launch Control Officer (LCO). The mated combination was called the MD-21. There were a total of two M-21 aircraft built, but one crashed after the aircraft collided with a D-21 during the launch. The only surviving one is available for viewing at Museum of Flight in Seattle, WA.

YF-12A: Prototype interceptor capable of launching air-to-air missiles. It had a pilot and a Fire Control Officer (FCO). Out of the three built, only one YF-12A is still in existance at the National Museum of the U.S. Air Force in Dayton, OH.

Trainer Variants

TA-12: The only A-12 trainer ever built is on display at California Science Center in Los Angeles, CA. The trainer has two cockpits: one for an instructor and one for a pilot in training. The plane is mostly bare-metal and hence is given the nickname "Titanium Goose".

SR-71B: The only remaining SR-71B in the world is located at Kalamazoo Air Zoo in Kalamazoo, MI. This aircraft is characterized by its raised rear-cockpit for the Instructor Pilot (IP) and by the two fins that are found under each engine nacelle.

SR-71C: There was only one C model Blackbird ever built, and it was the final SR-71 to be manufactured. It is located in Hill Aerospace Museum in Ogden,UT. The C model is a hybrid between the rear fuselage of the YF-12A and a functional engine mock up of a SR-71A forward fuselage built for static testing. The C model was built after the second of the two B model trainers crashed in January of 1968.
